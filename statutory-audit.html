Independent statutory audit services

Overview

Statutory audit, also known as external audit, is a type of audit that is mandated by a specific statute or law to make sure that the financial statements of the entities represent a true and fair view of its state of affairs and profitability. Statutory audit is governed by the Companies Act, 2013 and Companies (Audit and Auditors) Rules, 2014. The scope of the statutory audit mainly depends upon the guidelines and regulations set by the government. The financial statements and the accounts of the organization are scrutinized to avoid discrepancies or any possible misrepresentation and ensure reliability.
